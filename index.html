Celebrating 100 years in

Hello, and Welcome!

Welcome to the future. This year marks the Centennial of our Order and the beginning of a second century. This marks the beginning of tomorrow, an entire second century of Brotherhood, Cheerfulness, and Service leading us ever Onward and Upward, and I am pleased to welcome you to be a part of it.

Hosting Conclave at the Youngstown Air Reserve Station, we can look forward to many unique opportunities and activities never before seen at a Conclave. It is weekends like this Conclave, where you can try new things with your Brothers, that lead to the creation of memories that last a lifetime.

Speaking of Brotherhood, with each of the six lodges in our section gathering for this event, you will be able to meet other Arrowmen just like you from all over Eastern Ohio and parts of West Virginia.

I invite you to join me on this amazing adventure!

Michael Schloemer
Section C-4A Chief

Youngstown Air Reserve Station Vienna, Ohio

Conclave 2015 will be held at the Youngstown Air Reserve Station. It is located at:

3976 King Graves Road, Vienna, Ohio 44473

Ample signage will guide travelers in from State Route 11.

All persons attending Conclave must be listed on the Entry Authorization List (EAL). All Arrowmen registered will be added to the list. Others who need access should contact us.

Driving and staying with us

Arrowmen on the EAL should drive directly to the main gate at the Youngstown Air Reserve Station. Their identity will be confirmed, and then they will be provided access to the base. Once inside, service lodge personnel will direct you to the check-in location.

Drop-off

Arrowmen who are being dropped off at the event should proceed to the visitor center parking lot outside of the main gate. During limited hours service lodge personnel will provide a shuttle to the check-in location on the base. Hours will be published as the event nears. Outside of shuttle hours, Arrowmen will need to walk from the main gate to the designated check-in area.

A Letter to Parents

Dear Parents,

Congratulations on your son’s membership in the Order of the Arrow (OA). His election shows that the other members of his Boy Scout troop felt he exhibited Scouting’s principles and would give cheerful service to others. As you may know, most of the OA events in which your son will participate will take place locally with the OA lodge. Once each year, the six lodges in Northeast Ohio and Northern West Virginia (called a “section” or “Section C-4A”) gather for a fun-filled weekend called “Conclave.”

The 2015 C-4A Conclave will be held the weekend of May 15-17 at Youngstown Air Reserve Station (Vienna, OH), and is expected to be attended by over 225 Arrowmen from across our Section.

If your son has expressed an interest in attending the 2015 C-4A Conclave, consider allowing him to attend. Conclave is the premier event for both youth and adults and one of the highlights of the OA year! Conclave is a full weekend of activities and experiences designed to take Scouting to the next level for your son. A large group of Arrowmen from your local area will likely travel to Conclave together. When you complete the online registration process to sign-up for Conclave, you can indicate if your son needs a ride to Conclave and he will be contacted by someone from his local lodge to make those arrangements.

During the Conclave weekend, your son will experience specialized leadership training, learn more about the Order of the Arrow, have the opportunity to participate in friendly competitions, and have a lot of fun! This year, Conclave participants will also have the unique opportunity of exploring the Air Base and all it has to offer.

As you can see, the 2015 Conclave will be an action-packed and fulfilling event, just like it has been for more than fifty years. Your son is sure to have a fantastic weekend and grow as an individual and leader. He will have the chance to make new friends his age from across Ohio and West Virginia and make memories that will last a lifetime. We hope you will consider permitting your son to enjoy the 2015 C-4A Conclave. If you have any further questions about Conclave, be sure to visit the Frequently Asked Questions segment of this website or feel free to contact us.

Sincerely,
Sean McCabe
Section Adviser

Frequently Asked Questions FAQ

Housing

What options are available for housing at Conclave 2015?

Participants under 18 that are not staying in lodging with their parent or guardian will be housed in an assigned space within a building at the Youngstown Air Reserve Station. Based on availability, it is anticipated that this housing will primarily fall within the gym at the recreation center. Showers will be available. It would be acceptable to bring a cot to sleep on rather than on the floor. While the space will be divided to follow youth protection guidelines, there are no other dividers anticipated. Cots should be placed on a mat or have rubber feet in order to protect the floor on which they’re placed.

Those 18 years of age or older can also choose the included option described above or they can upgrade to the on base Eagle’s Nest Lodge (hotel) for an additional fee provided there is availability. Arrowmen under 18 may stay in a hotel room with their parent or guardian.

How much will it cost to upgrade to the Eagle’s Nest Lodge?

Suites will cost $69 per room per night. Rooms with two double beds or one queen bed will cost $60 per room per night. There is no applicable tax charged on the rooms.

What if I am arriving Saturday, can I stay in the Eagle’s Nest Lodge?

Yes, Saturday arrivals will be able to opt for a one night stay.

If staying in Eagle’s Nest Lodge, what time is check-out on Sunday morning?

Check-out is at 11am.

What is the process to confirm a stay in the Eagle’s Nest Lodge?

After registering for Conclave and selecting a hotel option, the reservation will be transmitted to the Eagle’s Nest Lodge. At the same time the room requester will receive an email with details on confirming the room with a credit card. If the room isn’t confirmed within a week it will be released back to the available inventory and the requester will be assigned included housing.

Can more than one person stay in a hotel room?

Yes, provided both Arrowmen are 18 years of age or older or an Arrowman under 18 is staying with his parent or guardian. Suites and double rooms have a limit of four people. Queen rooms are limited at two. One person will need to make the reservation and be the ‘owner’ of the room. He/she can add additional registered guests after confirming the hotel with the base. The email address of the person being added will need to be entered. The newly added guest will receive an email to confirm the switch from included housing to a hotel room. The ‘owner’ of the room must arrive before the guests will obtain access to the room. We make no assumptions about your preferred lodging arrangements; the roommate invitation procedure will need to be followed, even for adding family members.

Can I change my housing assignment once onsite?

No, final housing selections must be made prior to the closure of the registration system. Closure is anticipated on or near May 1, 2015. Per the Conclave Code of Conduct, participants must sleep in their designated housing area.

On-Base Policies

The Youngstown Air Reserve Station looks really large; can I drive my vehicle around the base?

Most Arrowmen will need to park their vehicle for the weekend in a central storage lot. For event logistics and medical purposes a limited number of driving passes will be issued by the Section Adviser and Service Lodge Adviser.

I will need to leave for a portion of the event. Will that be possible?

Yes. Once access has been provided to the base you will be able to enter and exit through the main gate.

Is there a Base Exchange (store) available?

Yes, the Base Exchange, known as the Shoppette, will be open during Conclave. It will offer an assortment of snacks, drinks and other merchandise. Alcohol and tobacco sales are prohibited to Conclave participants.

Registration & Check-In

Do I need to turn in a Medical form for Conclave?

YES! Everyone, that's EVERYONE MUST turn in a BSA medical form when they check-in. Please note: Medical forms will NOT be returned to Arrowmen, so please only submit copies that do not need to be returned. We respect everyone's privacy like our own, rest assured, your medical form will be shredded. You can download a med form here if you don't already have a valid copy.

What time can I arrive at the Youngstown Air Reserve Station?

Only Section & Service Lodge staff will be permitted on base prior to 6pm on Friday. PLEASE do not arrive earlier than 6pm if you have not received clearance from the Conclave Management Team or Service Lodge Leadership. Check-in will begin at 6pm.

How much does it cost to attend Conclave?

Well, it depends on when you register and pay. For details, see the registration section of this website.

How do I get to the Youngstown Air Reserve Station? Are there any special security procedures?

Please see the location section of this website. It provides door-to-gate directions along with information about getting on base.

I didn’t register before online registration closed. Can I still attend Conclave 2015?

No, sorry, due to security requirements for being on an active military installation we cannot accommodate walk-in registrations this year. We hope you’ll consider joining us this fall at the Area Leadership Training Conference or at Conclave 2016.

How can I pay my Conclave fee?

We offer four options to pay for Conclave: sending a check/money order, paying with a credit card (through PayPal), paying via your PayPal account, or bringing a check/cash with you to check-in. Remember, your fee is determined by when you register AND pay.

Do I need a PayPal account to use a credit card?

No! PayPal allows users to pay with a credit card without having an account. Just fill in your personal information and credit card details when redirected to PayPal and you'll be all set.

I didn't pay when I registered. What should I do?

You will be able to return to your registration to make modifications, add trading post items and pay later.

I have special medical needs. Should I still attend Conclave?

Absolutely! We will work to address all special needs that are noted during registration. Please make sure that you explain your specific need(s) in the appropriate fields.

What information do I need to register?

The following personal information is required at registration: first name, middle initial, last name, sex, date of birth, email address, lodge, honor level, address, and an emergency contact, their relationship to you, and their phone number. We will also ask you to indicate if you have mobility restrictions or special medical/dietary needs, which you may optionally answer to help us better service your needs, if any. We will also ask for your estimated time of arrival, estimated to the nearest half-hour, which helps us plan for the crowd and ensure we know not to consider you a no-show if you're getting in late.

Finally, we will ask your plans for lodging, transportation, and training. There are a few important notes about these selections. Please read the housing details to better understand the housing choices.

We will ask whether you are being dropped off, driving yourself, or riding with another driver to Conclave. These options will help us ensure adequate parking and permission for the vehicles that will be on base during Conclave. We will also offer the option of a coordinated carpool ride, where we will attempt to match you up with a driver from your lodge to get you to Conclave. This is not a guarantee of a ride, but we will do our best to fill every seat we have available from our volunteer drivers. We will confirm your ride and driver to you and provide contact information if we are able to match you with someone. If you choose the coordinated carpool, until you recieve a confirmation email, you do not have a carpool ride and you should keep alternate plans available.

Important Housing Information

We will offer multiple options for your lodging choice. The first option – 'included housing' – will be a space within a building on the Air Force base. Based on availability, we anticipate most will be housed in the recreation center. There will be access to showers. If this option is selected, it would be acceptable to bring a cot to Conclave rather than sleeping on the floor. Cots should be placed on a mat or have rubber feet in order to protect the floor on which they’re placed.

We will also offer the option to select a room within the Eagle’s Nest Lodge. This option is based on availability. The room inventory displayed in the registration system is accurate as of the time of registration. Suites are available for $69 per night (no tax) and rooms with either a queen bed or two double beds are available for $60 per night (no tax). Payment will be arranged through the Air Force. If this option is selected, an email will be sent with details on confirming your tentative hold on a room. Reservations will be required to be confirmed with a credit card within a week of email receipt, at which point you may be reassigned to 'included' housing automatically and your room released for someone else to reserve.

If you want to be added as a roommate to someone else's hotel room, the system will instruct you to choose 'included housing' as your lodging preference. This is important to ensure that you are considered to be available to be a roommate and that on the off-chance the room is already full, you won't be without a place to sleep. Arrowmen 18 years of age or older can be added to a confirmed room through the registration system. Arrowmen under 18 may stay be added to a room reserved by their parent or guardian. In order to stay in the hotel, it MUST be confirmed by the registration system. Suites and double rooms can accommodate 4 guests. Queen rooms can accommodate 2 guests. To be added to a room, the registered email address of the guest in question must be known. The person that reserved the room can add guest(s) by entering the guest’s email address in the roommate invitation form, which will be available only after the room is confirmed as reserved. The guest being added will receive an email asking them to accept or deny the invitation to be a roommate.

Section C-4A Conclave Code of Conduct

The general welfare of any group depends upon the conduct of each individual member. This Code of Conduct ensures the success of our Conclave and provides the maximum benefit to every participant. The Conclave leadership is responsible for the supervision of the event in respect to maintaining discipline and security, and every participant must agree to the following Code of Conduct:

  1. Observe the Scout Oath or Promise, the Scout Law and the Order of the Arrow Obligation. Follow the policies of the Boy Scouts of America including Youth Protection and Social Media. Adhere to directions given by base personnel or conclave leadership.
  2. Dress, according to the schedule, in either field uniform (Class A) or activity uniform (Class B) through the entire event. Wear the provided Conclave credential (name badge) when out of assigned housing area.
  3. The use of tobacco in any form, alcoholic beverages, illicit drugs, weapons of any kind including laser pointers, firearms, fireworks, or gambling is strictly prohibited.
  4. Participants and staff will be actively engaged in the Conclave program.
  5. Participants will be personally liable for any breakage, damage or loss of property.
  6. Participants must sleep in their designated housing area. Everyone must observe curfew and lights-out rules, and keep their sleeping area clean and tidy. The lodging (hotel) rooms may only be reserved by Arrowmen eighteen years of age or older. At no time should a standard room contain more than four people.
  7. Participants are not authorized to sell items at the event. The official trading posts (including the shoppette) are the only source for purchasing items at the event aside from lodge operated trading posts in assigned areas.
  8. Every participant must comply with all laws, as well as any special rules or regulations of the Youngstown Air Reserve Station, such as staying away from restricted and controlled areas.
  9. Once a car is parked for the event, it must remain in the designated parking lot until departure unless a driving pass has been issued by the Section Adviser or Conclave Service Lodge Adviser.
  10. Youngstown Air Reserve Station is a working military installation. Do not enter or disturb areas that are not explicitly defined in the Conclave program guide. There will be others on base during our visit, so it is of utmost importance that we respect their space and property.

The lodge adviser or contingent leader will approve the corrective measures for a violation of these rules before being implemented. Corrective measures will be at the discretion of the Conclave leadership, which may include dismissal from Conclave. The previous statement does not preclude the security forces of the Youngstown Air Reserve Station from taking action without intervention from Order of the Arrow leadership which may include dismissal from the base.

Cancellation Policy

Payments for registration and trading post fees are refundable less a 15% processing fee up until May 1. On or after May 1, there are no refunds. If a registrant does not attend Conclave, his or her packet (and trading post order, if applicable) will be provided to his or her lodge leadership for distribution.

Unpaid registrants have until May 1 to request a cancellation of their registration. If a cancellation request is not received, the registrant’s council may be billed for his or her outstanding fee(s).

Hotel reservations are cancellable per the policies of the Eagle’s Nest Lodge. Any cancellation request(s) for hotel reservations or registration must be initiated by using the “Contact Us” feature of the Conclave website (http://conclave.oa-c4a.org/).
